Our Work

There are many ways to get involved to giveback and make an impact on saving our marine life.

At Shark Attack, our work is fueled by the dedication of volunteers and supporters who believe in creating a positive impact on marine ecosystems. Together, we have achieved meaningful milestones that reflect our commitment to protecting marine life and promoting sustainable practices. Here are some of the key accomplishments we are proud to share:

Marine Wildlife Protection

  • Rescued and Released Over 1,500 Marine Animals: Through rescue efforts and partnerships with wildlife rehabilitation centers, we’ve helped injured sea turtles, seals, and other marine species return safely to their habitats.
  • Implemented Local Monitoring Programs:Volunteers actively monitor endangered species, such as sharks, dolphins, and sea turtles, contributing to scientific research and population conservation.

Beach Clean-Up

  • Removed Over 50 Tons of Ocean Waste:Through hundreds of beach clean-ups, our volunteers have cleared plastic, fishing gear, and other harmful debris from coastlines.
  • Partnered with Coastal Communities:We’ve built strong relationships with local communities, businesses, and schools to foster sustainable habits and promote pollution reduction.

Education & Advocacy

  • Reached 10,000+ People Through Workshops and Presentations:We’ve organized community events and educational programs to raise awareness about marine conservation and inspire environmental action.
  • Advocated for Policy Change:Shark Attack volunteers have supported petitions and campaigns that led to bans on harmful fishing practices and the reduction of single-use plastics in key regions.

Coral Reef Restoration Projects

  • Planted 5,000+ Coral Fragments:Our coral reef restoration efforts have rejuvenated damaged reefs, providing essential habitats for marine wildlife.
  • Developed Long-Term Restoration Sites:We’ve established ongoing projects where volunteers assist in monitoring reef health and biodiversity growth.

Global Volunteer Network

  • Built a Community of 2,500+ Active Volunteers Worldwide:Our network of passionate individuals continues to grow, creating a united force for marine conservation.
  • Collaborated with International Organizations:We’ve partnered with marine conservation groups around the world to amplify our efforts and share resources for greater impact.
